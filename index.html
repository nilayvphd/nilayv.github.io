About Me

Nilay is a 5th-year Ph.D. Candidate at Tufts University in the Biomedical Engineering department. Under the direction of Professor Irene Georgakoudi, Nilay's research is focused on application of machine learning to denoise low-SNR biomedical time-series and image datasets. His principal project has focused on label-free detection of rare circulating tumor cell clusters in whole blood using machine learning-based

classification algorithms. His secondary project has focused on denoising low-SNR clinical cervical biopsy images to improve image acquisition speed of a clinical probe for cervical pre-cancer detection. His future interest is in application of ML and DL for image super-resolution and denoising. He plans to pursue a career in industry after graduation in April 2023.
